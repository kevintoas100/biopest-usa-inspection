How to Complete the Inspection Report

A step-by-step guide for the technician on site

This guide walks you through the BioPest USA inspection report field by field. The goal of this visit is a fair, fact-based technical evaluation of the client's current pest control program at their bakery — what's working, what isn't, and what we would do better. Fill it in on your phone as you walk the site; it takes about 30–45 minutes.

ABefore you go

Bring: a phone (charged) with this link saved, a good flashlight, a telescoping mirror if you have one, gloves, a notepad as backup, and your ID / license.

💾 How saving works
Everything you type auto-saves inside the browser on the phone you're using. Use the same phone and same browser for the whole visit, and don't clear the browser history during the inspection. When you finish, you export a PDF — that PDF is what you keep and send. There is no login and nothing uploads to a server.

Set expectations with the client: explain you'll walk the whole facility, take photos of anything relevant, and ask to see their current provider's paperwork (service logbook, device map, product labels). Keep it professional and neutral.

BThe basics — how the form works

  • Text boxes: tap and type.
  • Colored chips (like ratings): tap to select. Tap the same one again to unselect.
  • Yes / No / N/A buttons: tap the one that applies. "N/A" = not applicable / couldn't check.
  • Photos: tap "Add photos" — you can take a new photo or pick from the gallery, several at a time.
  • Signatures: sign with your finger in the box.
  • When you're done, tap "Save as PDF" at the bottom.
🎯 Rule of thumb for ratings
Good / OK / Low Fair / Watch / Moderate Poor / Issue / High Critical
Green = acceptable, no action needed. Amber = minor issue, keep an eye on it. Red = real problem, needs correction. Use Critical only when there's active pest presence in a food area or a health/compliance risk.

1Inspection Details

The header info. Fill the client's business name and full address, the date, who met you on site, and your time in / time out. Put your name as the inspector and your license number if you have one. "Facility type" and "Scope" are pre-filled for a bakery audit — leave them unless something differs.

2Current Provider & Program Audit

This is where we evaluate the incumbent provider. Ask the client (or manager) who services them now, how often, and when they last came. Then ask to see their paperwork and mark the checklist:

  • Service logbook — is there a binder or app with visit records on site?
  • Application records — what product, where, how much, by whom.
  • Product labels & SDS — must be available for anything applied.
  • Applicator license — posted or on file.
  • Written IPM / scope plan, device map, and trend report — the marks of a serious program.
🚩 Common red flags to note
Empty or missing logbook · no device map · same generic "sprayed baseboards" every visit · expired or unlabeled product · stations not on any map · long gaps between visits · no trend/analysis. Write these in "Program observations."

3Pest Activity Assessment

Keep it simple — each pest has a traffic light. Tap one color; tap it again to clear it. Add the location in the optional notes box, and leave a pest untapped if you didn't check for it.

🚦 The traffic light
Green — None Yellow — Watch Red — Active
Green = no sign of this pest. Yellow = minor or old evidence, keep monitoring. Red = active, fresh, or widespread — live pests, fresh droppings, or infested product. When in doubt, take a photo (in the Photos section) and note what you saw.

See the pest cheat-sheet at the end for where each bakery pest hides and what its signs look like.

4Monitoring & Device Audit

Count and inspect the existing devices — this tells you a lot about the current provider.

  • Exterior rodent bait stations: count them; are they locked, anchored, labeled/dated, and is the bait fresh (not moldy or gone)?
  • Interior rodent devices (snap/glue/mechanical): are they placed along walls, accessible, and recently checked?
  • Insect light traps (ILTs / fly lights): count them, look for a bulb-change date (bulbs should be replaced ~yearly), and whether trays are clean. They should be away from food-prep surfaces and not visible from outside.
  • Pheromone / stored-product monitors: present? any catch?
🚩 Red flags
Unlocked or unanchored exterior stations · spoiled bait · devices that don't match any map · ILT bulbs years old · traps buried behind equipment where no one checks them.

5Facility Zone Inspection

Walk the site in a logical loop. For each area, rate it OK / Watch / Issue, write what you find, and use the "Add photo" button to attach photos right there in that zone. Suggested route:

  1. Exterior perimeter & foundation — gaps, vegetation against walls, harborage, station spacing.
  2. Receiving / dock — door seals, gaps under doors, dock levelers, incoming-product condition.
  3. Dry & ingredient storage (flour room) — the highest-risk area for stored-product pests. Check spillage, old product, webbing in corners.
  4. Mixing & prep — flour dust under and behind equipment, crumbs, moisture.
  5. Baking / oven area & proofing — warmth + moisture attract insects and drain flies.
  6. Cooling & packaging → finished-goods storage — product protection, cleanliness.
  7. Waste / dumpster area — a major attractant; lids closed, area clean, distance from doors.
  8. Break room, restrooms, roof & utilities — food debris, leaks, roof penetrations.

6Conducive Conditions

These are the things that invite pests. Use the Yes/No/N/A checklists — "Yes" means the good condition is met. Three groups:

  • Exclusion & structure: door sweeps, sealed gaps and utility penetrations, intact screens, self-closing doors.
  • Sanitation: clean floors and drains, no flour-dust buildup, equipment cleaned under/behind, no standing water, waste managed.
  • Storage: product off the floor, 18-inch wall clearance, FIFO rotation, sealed bulk containers, no damaged packaging.
✍️ Notes box
Summarize the biggest drivers here, e.g. "Flour dust accumulating under mixer line; gap under receiving door; two pallets stored directly on floor in dry storage."

7Overall Ratings

Give your professional read on structural/exclusion, sanitation, and the current provider's effectiveness, then an overall risk level. Finish with a 2–4 sentence executive summary — this is the first thing the client reads, so make it plain: what you found and the bottom line.

Example summary
"Overall pest pressure is moderate and mostly tied to sanitation and exclusion gaps in the flour and dock areas. The current provider's program is minimal and poorly documented — no device map, no trend records, and several unserviced stations. With a structured IPM program and targeted exclusion, these risks are readily controllable."

8Photo Evidence

Photograph anything you rate Watch/Issue or any pest evidence. For each photo, add a short caption (what it shows) and the location. Good photos to capture:

  • Gaps under doors / unsealed penetrations
  • Flour or debris buildup under equipment
  • Droppings, gnaw marks, webbing, insects, or damaged product
  • Device condition (open/unlocked stations, old ILT bulbs, dirty trays)
  • Overall shots of each key area
📷 Tip
Take the photo through the form's "Add photo" buttons so it attaches to the report. Wide shot for context, then a close-up of the detail. You can also add photos directly inside each Zone (section 5) and each Finding (section 9) — that keeps each photo next to what it shows.

9Findings & Recommendations

Tap "+ Add finding" for each issue. Give it a priority (High / Medium / Low), describe the finding, the recommended corrective action, and attach photos to that finding with the "Add photo" button. Keep each one specific and actionable.

Good example
Priority: High — "Fresh rodent droppings along the north wall of dry storage; gap under the receiving door allows entry." → Action: "Install a door sweep on the receiving door, seal the wall gap, and place two monitored snap stations along the runway; recheck in 7 days."

Then fill "BioPest USA proposed program" — how we'd service them (IPM approach, frequency, exclusion/sanitation work, monitoring, documentation and audit-readiness, next steps).

10Standards & Compliance Context

This section is mostly informational for the client (Illinois licensing, FDA Food Code / FSMA, third-party audits, EPA labeling). In the notes box, list any documentation or compliance gaps you found that tie back to those points — e.g. "No SDS on site," "Provider license not verifiable," "Not audit-ready: no device map or trend data."

11Sign-off

Sign in the Inspector box and type your name and date. If a client representative is present and willing, have them sign the Client acknowledgment box (this just confirms the visit happened — it is not an admission of anything).

CSave the report & send it

Tap "Save as PDF" at the bottom. Your phone's print screen opens — choose Save as PDF, then share it.

On iPhone

  1. Tap "Save as PDF" in the form.
  2. On the print preview, pinch out (zoom) on the page thumbnail to open it as a PDF.
  3. Tap the Share icon → Save to Files, or email / message it directly.

On Android

  1. Tap "Save as PDF" in the form.
  2. In the print dialog, set the destination to "Save as PDF" and tap the download button.
  3. Find it in Downloads and share via email / WhatsApp.
✅ Before you send
Open the PDF and scan it once — check the photos came through and the summary reads clearly. Then send it to the client (and keep a copy for BioPest USA).
